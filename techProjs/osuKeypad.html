OSU! KEYPAD

AUGUST, 2019
HO CHI MINH CITY, VIETNAM

Soldered, tested before putting keycaps on

With keycaps on

Previous Next

Osu! Keypad was a personal project that I made when I decided that I wanted a low-cost keypad that can serve my purpose of playing the game Osu! (which is a rhythm-based game that I really enjoy playing).

I looked up online and found an instruction that was available and did not cost me too much. Moreover, the owner of the instruction shared his PCB design and code so it was not much of a hassle for me to build. The switches that are currently soldered are Cherry MX Black. I am planning on making another one with Cherry MX Red (which will be uploaded soon) to test if it improves any performance on my Osu!-playing skills.
For this project, I used an Arduino Pro Micro and Arduino IDE to upload all the code into it. Works fine! Although, there was a problem with the PCB where the third switch from the left was not wired to TXO (according to the schematics) so I had to solder a jumper wire to connect them.
All credit goes to and further details can be found at: https://www.instructables.com/id/Osu-Keyboard/
